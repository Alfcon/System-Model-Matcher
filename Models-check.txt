Model Fit for Adult Chat (Capability vs Size)
1. Tone & Persona Control (most important)
Can the model:
	Stay in character over long chats
	Maintain a consistent personality/voice
	Adjust intensity (playful ↔ explicit) when asked

Requires strong instruction-following + roleplay stability

2. Context Memory (conversation continuity)
	Remembers details from earlier messages
	Tracks relationship dynamics, preferences, mood
	Doesn’t “reset personality” mid-chat

This is where mid–large models (7B–13B) are better over smaller ones

3. Language & Descriptive Ability
	Rich vocabulary (not repetitive)
	Natural dialogue flow
	Ability to vary pacing and wording

4. Responsiveness to Nuance
Can it:
	Pick up subtle cues
	Escalate or de-escalate tone naturally
	Handle emotional + conversational depth (not just explicit text)

5. Instruction Compliance
	Follows system prompts reliably
	Doesn’t randomly refuse or derail
	Handles custom rules (e.g. boundaries, style limits)

6. Stability (no weird breaks)
Watch for:
	sudden tone shifts
	going out of character
	nonsensical or looping replies

Capability vs Size (what actually matters)
Size	Behaviour
<7B	Fast, but shallow, repetitive
7B–9B	Best balance for chat/roleplay
13B+	More depth, slower, heavier
30B+	Overkill unless you need extreme realism

Instead of generic tests, use:
“Start a character-driven conversation”
“Maintain tone over 10+ messages”
“Adapt to changing emotional context”
“Avoid repetition over long replies”

Score:
immersion (1–10)
consistency (1–10)
natural flow (1–10)
Context Memory (1-10)
